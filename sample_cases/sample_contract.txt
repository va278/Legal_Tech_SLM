("Effective Date") by and between:

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Client desires to engage Contractor to develop certain software and provide related services;
WHEREAS, Contractor has the expertise and capacity to provide such services;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 1 — SCOPE OF SERVICES

1.1 Services. Contractor shall design, develop, test, and deliver a cloud-based inventory management
platform ("Software") in accordance with the specifications set forth in Exhibit A ("Specifications"),
incorporated herein by reference.

1.2 Change Orders. Any modification to the Specifications must be agreed in writing and may result in
additional fees and timeline adjustments at Contractor's then-current rates.

1.3 Subcontractors. Contractor may engage subcontractors without Client's prior written consent,
provided Contractor remains fully liable for all subcontractor work product and conduct.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 2 — PAYMENT TERMS

2.1 Fees. Client shall pay Contractor a fixed project fee of $480,000 USD, payable as follows:
    (a) 30% ($144,000) upon execution of this Agreement;
    (b) 30% ($144,000) upon delivery of Beta Version (estimated: June 1, 2025);
    (c) 40% ($192,000) upon Final Delivery and Client acceptance.

2.2 Expenses. Client shall reimburse reasonable, pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses within 30 days
of receipt of itemized invoices.

2.3 Late Payments. Invoices unpaid after 30 days shall accrue interest at 1.5% per month
(18% per annum), compounded monthly.

2.4 Taxes. Each party shall be responsible for its own taxes arising from this Agreement. Contractor
shall provide a completed W-9 upon request.

such incorporation at its discretion.

SECTION 4 — CONFIDENTIALITY

4.1 Definition. "Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one party
("Disclosing Party") to the other ("Receiving Party") that is designated as confidential or that

4.2 Obligations. Receiving Party shall: (a) hold Confidential Information in strict confidence using
at least the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential information (but no less than
reasonable care); (b) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written
consent; and (c) use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing obligations
under this Agreement.

4.3 Term. Confidentiality obligations shall survive for five (5) years after termination of this Agreement.

4.4 Exceptions. Obligations do not apply to information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available
without breach; (b) was already known to the Receiving Party; (c) is independently developed; or
(d) is required to be disclosed by law or court order, provided prompt written notice is given.

SECTION 8 — TERM AND TERMINATION

8.1 Term. This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until Final Delivery and
acceptance of the Software, unless earlier terminated.

8.2 Termination for Convenience. Client may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days' prior
written notice to Contractor, provided Client pays Contractor for all work completed through the date
of termination plus a termination fee equal to 15% of the remaining unpaid contract balance.

8.3 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate immediately upon written notice if the other
party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within fifteen (15) days
after written notice; or (b) becomes insolvent, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors,
or has a receiver appointed.

8.4 Effect of Termination. Upon termination, Contractor shall promptly deliver all completed work
product and works-in-progress to Client. Client shall pay for all services rendered through the
termination date. Sections 3, 4, 5.2, 6, 7, and 9 shall survive termination.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SECTION 9 — DISPUTE RESOLUTION

9.1 Informal Resolution. Prior to initiating formal proceedings, the parties shall attempt to resolve
any dispute through good-faith negotiation for thirty (30) days following written notice of the dispute.

9.2 Binding Arbitration. Any dispute not resolved informally shall be submitted to binding arbitration
administered by JAMS pursuant to its Comprehensive Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall be
conducted in San Francisco, California. The arbitrator's award shall be final and binding and may be
entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

9.3 Class Action Waiver. EACH PARTY WAIVES ANY RIGHT TO PARTICIPATE IN CLASS OR REPRESENTATIVE
PROCEEDINGS. Arbitration shall be conducted on an individual basis only.

9.4 Injunctive Relief. Notwithstanding Section 9.2, either party may seek emergency injunctive relief
in any court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm pending arbitration.
